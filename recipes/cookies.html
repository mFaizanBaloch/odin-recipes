Chocolate Chip Cookies Recipe

Description

These ultra-soft chocolate chip cookies are a must-make. Melted butter, extra brown sugar, a bit of cornstarch, and an extra egg yolk ensure the chewiest texture. Chill the dough before shaping. Check out all the details below!

Ingredients

  • 2 and 1/4 cups of all purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, melted & cooled 5 minutes
  • 3/4 cup packed light or dark brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1 and 1/4 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips or chocolate chunks

Steps

  1. Whisk together flour, baking soda, cornstarch, and salt in a large bowl.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk melted butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until smooth.
  3. Add egg, egg yolk, and vanilla extract to the wet ingredients and whisk until combined.
  4. Pour wet ingredients into dry ingredients and mix with a spoon or spatula until just combined.
  5. Fold in chocolate chips; dough will be soft and greasy.
  6. Cover dough tightly and chill in the refrigerator for 2 or 3 hours or up to 3 days.
  7. Remove dough from refrigerator and let soften at room temperature for 10 minutes.
  8. Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  9. Scoop 3 scant tablespoons for XL cookies or 2 heaping tablespoons for medium/large cookies; roll into tall balls.
  10. Place 8–9 dough balls per baking sheet; bake for 12 to 13 minutes until edges are lightly browned.
  11. Cool on baking sheet for 10 minutes; press extra chocolate chips on warm cookies if desired.
  12. Transfer cookies to wire rack to cool completely; store at room temperature for up to 1 week.
